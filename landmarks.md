## Eiffel Tower
**Wikipedia Summary**: The Eiffel Tower (/ˈaɪfəl/ EYE-fəl; French: [ɛfɛl]) is a wrought-iron lattice tower on the Champ de Mars in Paris, France. It is named after the engineer Gustave Eiffel, whose company designed and built the tower from 1887 to 1889 as the centerpiece of the 1889 World's Fair. Originally considered temporary, it was intended to be disassembled after 20 years but its height (330 metres, tallest structure in Paris) and utility for scientific experiments and radio transmissions saved it. Today, it is the most visited paid monument globally, welcoming around 7 million visitors annually, and a global cultural icon symbolizing Paris.
**Address**: Av. Gustave Eiffel, 75007 Paris, France
**Rating**: 4.7/5

## Louvre Museum
**Wikipedia Summary**: The Louvre Museum (French: Musée du Louvre), located in Paris, France, is the world's most-visited museum, receiving more than 8 million visitors annually. A central landmark of the city, it is situated on the Right Bank of the Seine in the 1st arrondissement (district). Its vast collection of art and artifacts spans over 800,000 objects, with around 38,000 on display, covering works from ancient civilizations to the mid-19th century. The museum occupies the Louvre Palace, originally built as a fortress in the late 12th century under Philip II and transformed into a royal palace by Francis I in the 16th century. Iconic works include the Mona Lisa, Venus de Milo, and Winged Victory of Samothrace.
**Address**: 75001 Paris, France
**Rating**: 4.7/5

## Arc de Triomphe
**Wikipedia Summary**: The Arc de Triomphe de l'Étoile is one of the most famous monuments in Paris, France. It stands 50 metres (164 ft) tall at the western end of the Champs-Élysées at the Place Charles de Gaulle, formerly named Place de l'Étoile. The monument honours those who fought and died for France in the French Revolutionary and Napoleonic Wars, with the names of all French victories and generals inscribed on its inner and outer surfaces. Commissioned in 1806 after the victory at Austerlitz by Emperor Napoleon I, who may have envisioned it as a counterpart to the Arc de Triomphe du Carrousel; its iconic design was finalized by Jean Chalgrin. Construction was interrupted after Napoleon's fall but completed in 1836 under King Louis-Philippe. Beneath its vault lies the Tomb of the Unknown Soldier from the First World War.
**Address**: Pl. Charles de Gaulle, 75008 Paris, France
**Rating**: 4.7/5
